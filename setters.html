Setter

The Quarterback of the Team

Position Overview:

The setter is the quarterback of the team. They are essential in running the offense, and make sure that their hitters get a good ball to spike. This position gets the most touches and is often the most difficult position to play well.

Tips on being a great Setter

  • Square to your Target

    • When setting the ball to your hitters, you need to adjust your body to square up to your target. What does that mean? As you are volleying, you want to be facing straight toward the location you are sending the ball.

  • Use Your Legs to Set

    • When you consistently use your legs to generate a large amount of power for the set, that spreads out the demand on body. The power of your sets comes from 3 places: your legs, your elbows, and your wrists.

  • Flexible, But Firm Hands

    • When you’re setting, your hands need to be firm and flexible. The correct hand position is about 1 ½ inches apart at the thumbs and index fingers. Your hands should form a triangle. Your hands should also be in the shape of the ball. As the ball arrives, your hands and fingers will absorb the ball and then send it out to your target.
